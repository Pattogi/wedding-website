Intro


In December 2019, Logi travelled to Bournemouth to visit a friend, and there he met Patty. Quickly becoming good friends, Logi and Patty spent much of the early days of lockdown shooting eachother off of cliffs and setting eachother on fire (thankfully no hospital trips required, they were playing Magicka).
Fast forward a few months, Logi and Patty were spending as much time as possible wandering around forests, being silly and falling in love.
After their mid-forest handfasting ceremony in 2023, they decided to make it official and plan their wedding.

Dress code

Hobbit / Fantasy attire
Our wedding will have a Hobbit / LOTR / Fantasy theme, and we would love for you to get involved too to create a unique, fairytale-like atmosphere. Want to be a hobbit or an elf for a day? A powerful wizard? A greedy dragon? A mighty warrior? Maybe a flower fairy? Please, go ahead, we would love to see it!

If you are not too sure of what this means or what to wear, feel free to ask us for inspiration. You will most likely already have things at home you can use and, if not, charity shops are a great place to look for them

Nothing is too crazy or too silly for us, as long as you are comfortable and have a good time. The only thing we would say is, feel free to leave your fancy dress and heels at home!

Food

What about second breakfast?

This won't be your usual wedding in terms of food either, so please read carefully!

We will be putting some sharers and snacks for all tastes, but the star dish will be yours to choose!

If you are able, please bring something you have made yourself to share with everyone, extra points if it's a typical dish from your country or area.
Ideally, bring food for at least one person each, ideally two. The more variety we can get, the better.

We love food, we love variety, we love sharing, and the meals we have enjoyed the most have been the ones made with love by our loved ones. Trust us, it's going to be amazing :)

Note: We will provide tags for the dish names and any extra information (vegan or vegetarian, allergens...). We will also have a list of dishes to make sure none of them are repeated too many times.

How do I get there?

It's way easier than you think!

If you don't own a car, you can get there by public transport from Bournemouth in about 2 hours by taking a train to Weymouth and then a bus that will leave you near the gardens.
